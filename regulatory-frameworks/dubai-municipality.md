# Dubai Municipality (DM) Requirements

**Authority**: Dubai Municipality (DM)
**Scope**: Building codes, zoning regulations, master plan compliance, NOC issuance
**Website**: https://www.dm.gov.ae

## Zoning Classifications

### Commercial Zones
- **C1**: Local commercial (neighborhood retail, small offices)
- **C2**: General commercial (shopping centers, business districts)
- **C3**: Heavy commercial (warehouses, logistics, wholesale)
- **C4**: Mixed-use commercial (retail + residential + office)

### Industrial Zones
- **I1**: Light industrial (assembly, packaging, small manufacturing)
- **I2**: General industrial (medium manufacturing, logistics hubs)
- **I3**: Heavy industrial (petrochemicals, heavy machinery - restricted locations)

### Special Zones
- **MU**: Mixed-use development (integrated live-work-play)
- **H**: Hospitality (hotels, serviced apartments, tourism facilities)
- **R**: Retail (shopping malls, entertainment complexes)

## Building Code Requirements

### Plot Coverage Ratio (PCR)
- **Commercial C1/C2**: 60% max coverage
- **Commercial C3**: 70% max coverage
- **Industrial I1/I2**: 75% max coverage
- **Mixed-Use**: 65% max coverage

### Floor Area Ratio (FAR)
- **Commercial C1**: FAR 2.0 (e.g., 1000 sq m plot = 2000 sq m total built-up area)
- **Commercial C2**: FAR 3.0
- **Commercial C3**: FAR 2.5
- **Industrial I1/I2**: FAR 1.5
- **Mixed-Use**: FAR 2.5-4.0 (depends on zone and master plan alignment)

### Setback Requirements
- **Front setback**: 6m minimum from plot boundary
- **Side setback**: 3m minimum (or 10% of building height, whichever is greater)
- **Rear setback**: 6m minimum
- **Exception**: Zero setback allowed in designated high-density commercial corridors (e.g., Business Bay, DIFC)

### Height Restrictions
- **Low-rise zones**: 4 floors max (G+3)
- **Mid-rise zones**: 12 floors max (G+11)
- **High-rise zones**: 40+ floors (subject to aviation clearance and structural engineering approval)

## NOC (No Objection Certificate) Process

### Timeline
- **Fast-track**: 2-3 weeks (for standard commercial developments with complete documentation)
- **Standard**: 4-6 weeks (for complex mixed-use or industrial projects)
- **Complex**: 8-12 weeks (for high-rise, special zones, or projects requiring Planning & Survey Department review)

### Required Documents
1. **Plot ownership proof** (title deed or lease agreement)
2. **Approved architectural drawings** (stamped by registered consultant)
3. **Structural engineering report** (for buildings >4 floors)
4. **MEP (Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing) drawings**
5. **Traffic impact assessment** (for developments >10,000 sq m or >200 parking spaces)
6. **Environmental impact study** (for industrial or high-density projects)
7. **NOCs from other authorities**:
   - Civil Defence (fire safety)
   - DEWA (utilities connection)
   - RTA (parking and transport)
   - ESMA (if food facility)
   - Aviation Authority (if near airport flight paths)

### Fees
- **Building permit**: AED 25-50 per sq m of built-up area
- **NOC processing**: AED 5,000-20,000 (depends on project complexity)
- **Consultant registration**: AED 10,000 (one-time, if not already registered)

## Master Plan Compliance

### Dubai 2040 Urban Master Plan Alignment
DM enforces conformance with Dubai 2040 strategic objectives:

1. **Urban Centers**: Properties in designated centers (Business Bay, Dubai Marina, DIFC) receive priority approvals
2. **Economic Corridors**: Developments along Sheikh Zayed Road, Al Khail Road, Emirates Road qualify for expedited processing
3. **Transformation Zones**: Areas earmarked for redevelopment (Al Quoz, Deira) subject to stricter design guidelines
4. **Green Building Standards**: All new commercial buildings >5,000 sq m must achieve Al Sa'fat (Dubai Green Building) rating

### Rezoning Risk Assessment
DM updates zoning maps every 3-5 years based on Dubai 2040 implementation:

- **High Risk (>50% probability)**: Industrial zones near planned Metro extensions, Al Quoz transformation area
- **Medium Risk (20-50%)**: Commercial zones in Dubai Marina, Business Bay (densification pressure)
- **Low Risk (<20%)**: Established free zones (JAFZA, DMCC), designated commercial corridors

## Common Rejection Reasons

1. **FAR exceedance** (requesting 4.0 in a 2.5 zone) - **60% of rejections**
2. **Insufficient parking** (not meeting RTA ratios) - **25% of rejections**
3. **Non-compliant building height** (aviation conflicts) - **10% of rejections**
4. **Environmental impact concerns** (industrial emissions, noise) - **5% of rejections**
